Getting domain name and web hosting

Getting Domain Name

As you already know, registering your domain name is the first step in establishing your online presence and reaching visitors. Registering a domain name means registering the name you want with an organisation called ICANN through a domain name registrar.

To get a domain name you have to pay a registration fee to domain name registrar. That will give you the right to the domain name for a year, and you will have to renew it annually for (usually) the same amount per annum. Purchasing a name is a relatively simple process (although finding one that isn't already taken can be a challenge), but there are many factors that you should consider. Since your domain name is, in effect, the name of your website, you want to make sure you get a good one.

Characteristics of good domain name:

  • Simple and understandable for your target audience
  • Easy to remember
  • Can be on the name of your business or keywords of your product, services or blog
  • Avoid hypes and dash [Remember: domain name never accepts space between the characters]

Domain names disappear extremely fast. Many people claim that all the good domain names are gone. It is probably true that most good domain names that are descriptive of products and services have been taken. If you want a domain name for your site, I suggest you act now, or face the anguish of having lost that name later.

Suggestion: Think of a few good domain names that you'd like to use. Don’t just think one domain name. It might already be taken. So think multiple domain names.
